In the agile space, a team is performance unite. A performance unit is an encapsulated body and its mission is to transform backlog into a valuable product. Team psychology has a great role to play to determine its velocity (Goodpasture 2010: 189). Also, the mental character of group plays a vital role to meet and fulfill all the values and principles of the agile manifesto. Team psychology enables to examine the crucial factors in the growth and development of an effective team. Introducing organizational concepts and offering evidence-based intervention to enhance the performance of a whole team. Team psychology has been determined based on theory of team psychology in a work. A theory in psychology is an organized collection of ideas that serves to describe, explain or predict what a person thinks, feels or do (Arnold, Randall, and Patterson 2010: 78). To be successful it needs to specify the five elements given below. ● ● The particular behavior, thoughts or emotion of an individual. Any differences between people in a degree to which they characteristically exhibit the thoughts, behavior and emotions. ● ● Situational factors that might influence thoughts, behavior and emotions. Any consequences of the interaction between given theory 2 and 3 for the thoughts, behavior and emotions. ● Occurrence of particular thoughts, behavior or emotions that might feedback to produce change in 2 and 3 and have a positive impact on a team. Based on this theory, we were able to see, predict and solve the occurrence of late arrival Fig: 1 A simplified structure of psychological theory. (Coon 2017: 135) for stand-up meeting. We initially predicted that the relevant individual characteristics might include the degree of a person’s work satisfaction and stable traits like the extent to which a team to be well organized. Relevant past experience might include a penalty for being late. Situational features might include distance, simplicity and complexity to travel from home to the meeting area, weather and traffic jam. These situations might have a direct impact on a person’s actual incidence of arriving late. On the other hand, we put thought on the interaction between person and situation. For example, a well-organized person may have no trouble or difficulty making complex travel. So, here the issue was the lack of well organizational skill of a person. It's likely that person and situation influence each other. At first, we noticed it occurring in at least three ways. First, a person’s character affecting the types of situation exposed by him/herself. For example, not deciding carefully on where to live. Second, People changing the features of their situation, or vice versa. Third, a person’s behavior leading him\her to change and make a well-organized person or changing situations for example, being dismissed for being late once again. Approaches to work motivation Motivation and how to constantly manage it is the measure concern while accomplishing any goal. To complete this agile project, we looked at the concept of motivation, explored some of its implications and implemented some of the theories in practice. Need theories, Expectancy theory, Goal-setting theory, intrinsic motivation, extrinsic motivation and self-determined theory. Need theory This theory is based on the idea that there are psychological needs probably of biological origin. In case of our needs are unmet, we experience disequilibrium after which we try to put right. Examining this theory with practices we found that the notion of need reflects the content of motivation. In general psychology the notion of need has a long history. The best known of this theory is Abraham Maslow. He was humanistically oriented psychologist and later offered general theory of human functioning. Later, he refined andproposed five classes of human needs which are shown in hierarchy (Coon 2017: 119). After going through this theory, it made us clear that it concentrates on the process. This choice process is seen as cognitive. Based on this theory and following three factors we chose possible courses of action we would pursue. Three factors include expectancy, instrumentality and valence. Expectancy: If I tried, would I be able to perform the task which I’m considering? Instrumentality: Would performing the task lead to identifiable outcomes? Valence: How much do I value outcomes? Goal-setting theory Expectancy, instrumentality and valence was not enough in order to accomplish the task. First phase was knowing own capacity and worth of the task. As Locke et al defines, ‘Goal is what an individual trying to accomplish; it is an aim of an action. (Locke et al. 1981: 128)’ And goal setting is like a bridge to bring idea or aim into action. In the processes of trying to figure out that one of the factors was due to the lack of intrinsic motivation. Deci and Ryan (2008) explains, ‘intrinsic motivation is a doing of an activity for its inherent satisfaction’. Whereas, ‘extrinsic motivation pertains that an activity is done in order to obtain some separable outcomes’ . We first gave priority to extrinsic motivation but later on we found missing intrinsic one. In one research, Herzberg argued that intrinsic motivation is more powerful and reliable than extrinsic motivation as a way of influencing behavior at work (Grigaliunas and Herzberg 1971). Agile Processes Agile software development refers to a variety of software development methods that promotes continuous iteration of development and is based on iterative development throughout the software development lifecycle (Goodpasture 2010). Requirements and solutions are found using collaboration between the members of the teams. It helps to assist the team to respond to the unpredictability while developing software. It applies incremental and iterative progression segments that are known as sprints. Our Team Scrum team comprises of 7+/-2 members. There is no definite team leader to assign tasks or decide how to solve a problem, as the team members are self-organized. Scrum Methodology Figure 8: Comparison Agile Project management methodology (Qumer and Henderson-Sellers 2008) We applied scrum project management methodology to our project. It is an agile project management framework which is especially used for software development. It follows thevalues and principles from agile but involves further specifics particularly regarding certain software development practises (Rubin 2012). Sprint planning A sprint is a time period during which a specified amount of work is done and prepared for review. Our sprints lasted a week with five working days and slack time of two days. Saturday and Sunday were left for slack and other five days as working days. Sprint planning is the meeting done to determine which product backlog story will be delivered in that sprint. We conducted sprint planning every Monday when the sprint ended or after the sprint reviews and retrospectives from the previous sprint. Scrum master facilitated to make sure the discussion is effective and appropriate product backlog items are included in the sprint backlog. The Daily Stand-up meeting It is a short meeting for communication between members not more than 15 minutes where every member covers the development since last meeting. Planning work and impediments are discussed that may be blocking the development of a member in daily stand-up meeting (Cohn 2009: 72.). Most of our stand up lasted only for about 15-20 minutes and done in exact time of 10 AM everyday. The sprint review: It is the event to demonstrate and present the work of the team done during the sprint. Product owner checks if the work done meets the acceptance criteria and either accept it or reject it. The stakeholders reviews the work and give feedback to ensure the development meets the requirements. The sprint review was done at the end of the sprint every monday and reviewed by our teacher. The retrospective It is the final meeting at the end of every sprint to discuss and find what went well and didn’t go well or how it can be improved in another sprint. It was held after the sprint review. We were supposed to identify elements of the process that did or did not work during a sprint. We facilitated this meeting by asking the following questions (Derby and Larsen 2006: 129). ● What went well? ● What would we like to change ? ● How can we improve change? Scrum Artifacts Product Backlog It is a document or list that outlines everything that is needed in the product to complete or it can also be explained as requirements for the product (Schwaber 2007: 58). Backlog is managed accordingly in terms of their value proposition by the product owner. While creating product backlog we focused on two foundations which is road map and requirements. Roadmap initiatives got breakdown into several epics with each epic having several user stories and requirements. All working items like user stories, customer requests, design changes were included in the product backlog Sprint Backlog It is a specific list of product backlog items which are to be finished in that certain sprint. During the sprint planning meeting we selected some number of product backlog items in order to identify necessary task to be accomplished.
